Appendix: 75 Territories

The Diplomacy map feels crowded. Because it is.

Of the 75 spaces, 34 are supply centers.

Many spaces aren’t used that often. Perhaps 50 territories are important (to varying degrees), with the remaining 24 used situationally.

What follows is merely (primarily?) for amusement.

‘SC’ to indicate supply center.

‘HSC’ to indicate home supply center.

Landlocked or Coastal Provinces

Bohemia: Boh

Alongside Tyrolia, this territory separates Austria and Germany. Borders Munich, Vienna, Silesia, and Galicia as well.

Budapest: Bud (HSC)

Key Austria home center bordering two out of the four Balkan neutral supply centers (Serbia and Rumania).

Galicia: Gal

Usually an important point of discussion for the Archduke and Czar in 1901 as it borders two Austria home centers and Rumania.

Trieste: Tri (HSC)

Austria’s only coastal home center.

Tyrolia: Tyr

Accessible in Spring 1901 by Austria, Germany, and most usefully by Italy.

Vienna: Vie (HSC)

As Austria, are you opening to Trieste, Galicia, or Budapest?

Clyde: Cly

Borders English home centers Edinburgh and Liverpool. Sparsely visited.

Edinburgh: Edi (HSC)

Northern English home center bordering the Norwegian Sea and the North Sea.

Liverpool: Lvp (HSC)

English home center. Also England’s side door, often visited by France via F MAO-IRI.

London: Lon (HSC)

Building Fleets here gives more flexibility than Edinburgh.

Wales: Wal

Useful for attacking and defending the island.

Yorkshire: Yor

Borders all three English home centers. Open A Lvp-Yor unless you are sure France won’t be in the English Channel.

Brest: Bre (HSC)

One of two French coastal home centers, bordering the critical Mid-Atlantic Ocean and English Channel.

Burgundy: Bur

Pivotal territory for French defense and access to Belgium, Ruhr, and Munich.

Gascony: Gas

Defensive territory bordering Brest, Paris, Marseilles, and Spain. Featured in the Iberian Indecision opening.

Marseilles: Mar (HSC)

Fleet builds here are not necessarily pro-English.

Paris: Par (HSC)

French home center with good spacing.

Picardy: Pic

French armies show up here when the President wishes to guarantee influence on Belgium without irritating Germany in Burgundy. English armies may arrive here by convoy as well.

Berlin: Ber (HSC)

German home center available for both army and fleet builds.

Kiel: Kie (HSC)

Important German home center bordering neutrals Denmark and Holland. Available for both army and fleet builds.

Munich: Mun (HSC)

Arguably THE center. Germany’s most important home center. Access to 8 adjacent and active territories. Featured in Richard Sharp’s Anschluss theory.

Prussia: Pru

Just south of the Gulf of Bothnia, this territory sees utility in northern conflicts.

Ruhr: Ruh

Defensive and offensive territory for Germany, bordering Munich, Burgundy, Holland, and Belgium.

Silesia: Sil

Critical mid-game territory between Germany, Russia, and Austria. Stalemate importance.

Apulia: Apu

Borders Venice, Naples, and the Ionian. Common to see an army here defensively or for potential convoys (Tunis; Albania).

Naples: Nap (HSC)

Italian home center and most reliable place for a Winter 1901 fleet build.

Piedmont: Pie

Land route between France and Italy. With Switzerland, it is key for stalemate lines.

Rome: Rom (HSC)

Coastal home center. Building a fleet here has less flexibility than F Nap, but if you’re building two…

Tuscany: Tus

Borders Rome, Venice, and Piedmont.

Venice: Ven (HSC)

Italian home center bordering Austria’s home center, Trieste.

Livonia: Lvn

With an army, borders StP, War, Pru, and Mos. Also see the Livonian Lunacy opening.

Moscow: Mos (HSC)

Key Russian home center. In the early and mid-game it is useful for positioning and influence, but in the north and south.

Sevastopol: Sev (HSC)

If Russia can clear this home center, they might find use for a second southern fleet.

St. Petersburg: StP (HSC) (north & south coasts)

For fleet builds and fleet movement, specify your coast!

Ukraine: Ukr

Useful defensive and setup territory for Russia.

Warsaw: War (HSC)

Russian home center connected to Moscow and with access to Prussia, Silesia, Galicia, and Ukraine.

Ankara: Ank (HSC)

Turkish home center bordering the pivotal Black Sea.

Armenia: Arm

As Turkey, useful leverage against Sevastopol.

Constantinople: Con (HSC)

The Bosporus creates a defensive bottleneck, but until the sultan has a fleet in the Aegean or the Black Sea, it will be difficult to mobilize armies. May be traversed by armies and fleets.

Smyrna: Smy (HSC)

I hear Antalya is quite pleasant. The Italians have heard that too.

Syria: Syr

Where the Italians may opt to convoy if they can’t get into Smyrna.

Albania: Alb

Another potential convoy landing zone for Italy.

Belgium: Bel (SC)

Of interest to England, France, and Germany.

Bulgaria: Bul (SC) (south and east coast)

Turkey’s 100% in Spring 1901.

Denmark: Den

Neutral center often occupied by Germany. Similar to Constantinople, can be traversed by armies and fleets.

Finland: Fin

Comes into play in northern skirmishes.

Greece: Gre (SC)

Often Austria’s to begin with, but the Italian and Turk have their say as well.

Holland: Hol (SC)

Germany can guarantee Holland in 1901, and often claims it even if opening to Denmark.

Norway: Nwy (SC)

Usually scooped by England (with a fleet or army), and creates negotiation opportunities given its bordering Russia’s Saint Petersburg. (Russia can’t access it in 1901 given its fleet begins the game on Saint Petersburg’s south coast.)

North Africa: Naf

Can be important for the MAO/WES/SPA stalemate line.

Portugal: Por (SC)

Easily accessible neutral center for France.

Rumania: Rum (SC)

One of the four highly contested Balkan centers. Russia can guarantee it in 1901, but only with concessions positionally.

Serbia: Ser (SC)

Balkan center usually claimed by Austria.

Spain: Spa (SC)

Neutral center easily collected by France. For fleet movement, specify north and south coasts!

Sweden: Swe (SC)

A topic of discussion for Germany and Russia.

Tunis: Tun (SC)

Will Italy secure it with a fleet or army in 1901?

Bodies of Water

Adriatic Sea: ADR

Austrian home center and only Austrian salt water access.

Aegean Sea: AEG

Influence on the Ionian, Greece, Bulgaria (south coast), Constantinople, Smyrna, and the Eastern Mediterranean.

Baltic Sea: BAL

Borders two German home centers as well as Denmark and Sweden.

Barents Sea: BAR

Can be used by the English to support themselves into Saint Petersburg.

Black Sea: BLA

Borders five supply centers.

Eastern Mediterranean: EAS

Featured in Edi Birsan’s Lepanto opening.

English Channel: ENG

If you are England, consider opening here, if for no other reason but to keep the French out.

Gulf of Bothnia: BOT

The way by sea into Saint Petersburg’s south coast.

Gulf of Lyon: GOL

Borders Marseilles, Spain (south coast), Piedmont, Tuscany, the Tyrrhenian Sea, and the Western Mediterranean.

Helgoland Bight: HEL

Imagine if this space didn’t exist. Does England or Germany do better?

Ionian Sea: ION

Often controlled for some time by Italy, but the President, Archduke, and Sultan would sure love to own it as well.

Irish Sea: IRI

England’s side door.

Mid-Atlantic Ocean: MAO

Critical for France’s mobility, defense, and attack.

North Atlantic Ocean: NAO

Sometimes used to support movement into the Mid-Atlantic Ocean.

North Sea: NTH

Borders 6 supply centers. Whichever Great Power controls it increases his potential greatly.

Norwegian Sea: NWG

England may open here to give themselves the option to convoy an army to Norway in Fall 1901 with either NWG or NTH.

Skagerrak: SKA

A very useful space. For The Skagerrak Sentry opening, see Strategy.

Tyrrhenian Sea: TYS

Access to two Italian centers.

Western Mediterranean: WES

Critical in French/Italian conflict and on end-game stalemate lines.
