Acknowledgments

Bill Self for introducing me to the game in his high school history class, where we played the game in groups for a project grade.

The students at Cy Creek who enthusiastically joined the Diplomacy Club I set up. I remember well the quick negotiations we squeezed in between classes. (We had twice-weekly moves on a board in a spare classroom.)

For my competitors online on the DPjudge.

Ben Johnson, Zachary Moore, and Jake Langenfeld, who brought me into the Minnesota Diplomacy Club’s council.

Jake, again, who mentioned at one of our meetings that he dreams of writing a Diplomacy openings book. I thought I did, too, but in setting out (to beat him to the punch) I realized an openings manual wasn’t my book to write, and I was being pulled by something different. What came of those musings is the book now in your hands.

To Chris Martin for his excellent YouTube series recapping the WDC 2016 at WeaselMoot in Chicago.

To my early readers for their feedback.

To the Diplomacy hobby and community with which I hope to more fully entangle myself.

“All my friends were once strangers. And then I invited them to Diplomacy and we ceased to be friends. But as they could not revert to ‘stranger’ status…they became…acquaintances.”
